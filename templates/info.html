Accommodation

We organise and arrange accommodation to suit the needs of various delegates. We are associated with reputable hotels and guest houses to provide a good bed & breakfast accommodation at discounted rates. Contact us for rates.

Transport

Our vehicles provide free transport during excursions and hospitality tours, to and from the Airport, to and from the lecture venues if this is not practically feasible within a walking distance.

Travel, Visas and Student's Pass

All course delegates attending a training in Swaziland require a student's pass (visa). This takes a day to be processed at a cost that is included in the tuition fee and it is processed before course delegate(s) arrives in Swaziland. All public/ scheduled programmes include hospitality tours within Mozambique, Namibia, South Africa or Swaziland. These countries are visa free for Tanzania Citizens. We advise all delegates from other countries to consult the respective embassies in their home countries for visa formalities before leaving for Swaziland.

Living Expenses

The official currency in Swaziland is a Swazi Lilangeni (SZL) which is an equivalent to a South African Rand (ZAR). The South African currency is also used in Swaziland. People coming to Swaziland from overseas generally find the cost of living comparatively low due to the depreciated local currency. Restaurant prices vary depending on the type of establishment. A good meal for one at an average restaurant costs around R150, top category establishments can cost up to R200. These prices are based on one course and a soft drink or a beer. However, hotels and restaurants charge tax in addition to the prices shown for meals and accommodation. Although most prices are tax inclusive, services are also subject to VAT at a rate of 15%.

Weather

Climate in Swaziland varies according to altitude, with the higher areas being generally cloudy and cooler than the rest of Swaziland. The rainy season is generally between November and April and is also humid and wet (Summer). And the cooler, drier conditions are generally between May and October (Winter).
